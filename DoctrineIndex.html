The following Table of Contents is to the Doctrine Books, I, II, and III of the eight volume set of The Works of George Fox. These doctrine books are full of outstanding writings, which are significant help in understanding the Bible, the Old Covenant, the New Covenant, and the true hope of the scriptures. The editor of this site has found some of these writings breath-taking in the understanding and hope presented, backed up with great depth of scriptures quoted. Fox's writings have been edited for modernized language, grammar, and punctuation. Many of Fox's words are directly from the King James Bible,* and many of them are linked for your reference and enjoyment.

*Fox used the King James Bible of the 17th Century. There have been many minor clarifications and corrections to the King James version since then. You may notice an occasional slight difference in a word Fox is quoting from the older King James Version compared to today's King James Version, but never of any significance. In the KJV, conversation meant conduct, charity meant love; these changes have been made in Fox's quotations. On a several occasions, Fox's direct quotations from the King James have been replaced by more understandable text from the New King James Bible; or less frequently, the New International or Amplified Bibles.

If you would like to read more about who George Fox was, the 1st Chapter of his Journal is particularly recommended, which describes his youth and spiritual journey. His inspiring translation to paradise is detailed and concludes with being raised by Christ up to the state of Adam and Eve before the fall, still capable of falling to temptation and then raised to the state of Christ, to sit down in Christ, beyond Adam's state and not subject to falling to temptation.

In addition, there is also available a short summary of his life and the early Quakers that he founded.

These writings are almost all addressed to the spiritually mature Christians, those who had experienced or were on the journey's completion to the baptism of death, Christ to be risen within them, to see his face, to fellowship with He and the Father, and to be living in paradise — while still on earth. These letters are full of spirit breathed exhortations and encouragements.
