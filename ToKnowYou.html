And this is eternal life, that they may know You,
the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent.
John 17:3

If we are to know Him, we cannot just read the writings of others who have been touched by Him;
we must come to Him, and be taught by Him about himself, as in humble silence we wait on Him who is within each of us.
Here He will reveal himself to us in a very personal way. And He will show us ourselves,
so we can repent and be changed — and He can know us.

George Fox once said that thirty minutes in the light is worth a lifetime.
It is our life breath who love Him.

David also loved this waiting on God:
Be still, and know that I am God Psa 46:10

This wonderful quest is the blessing of this lifetime —and forever —to become more deeply and intimately acquainted with Him. As Paul- who had no writings said:
I count everything as loss compared to the possession of the priceless privilege of knowing Christ Jesus My Lord, and of progressively becoming more deeply and intimately acquainted with Him. Phl 3:8

For my determined purpose is that I may know Him [that I may progressively become more deeply and intimately acquainted with Him, perceiving and recognizing and understanding the wonders of His Person more strongly and more clearly], and that I may in that same way come to know the power outflowing from His resurrection [which it exerts over believers], and that I may so share His sufferings as to be continually transformed [in spirit into His likeness even] to His death Phl 3:10

And he said, The God of our forefathers has destined and appointed you to come progressively to know His will [to perceive, to recognize more strongly and clearly, and to become better and more intimately acquainted with His will], and to see the Righteous One (Jesus Christ, the Messiah), and to hear a voice from His own mouth and a message from His own lips Acts 22:14

He died on the cross to show us how to die on our spiritual cross.
He is the pattern Son, to show us how to die spiritually to self.
And the cross to us is nothing compared to his. It is easy and light.
When we sit and wait on him and watch for at least an hour, our will is to get up and do something else.
When He shows us our condition, our will is to run or deny that we are that bad.
When He commands to give up doing certain things, our will is to keep doing them.
So when we do the opposite of our will and repent, we are on the cross, dying to self.
Our death is a spiritual death.
To share the cross with Christ, however light ours is.
To share death with Christ, however easy ours is.
To share the resurrection with Christ, both to his glory.
Here we can be taught by Him, led by Him, changed by Him, and come to Know Him.
We see the effects of the changes in us- with wonder and loving thanksgiving,
and we begin to understand why he came and what he is doing.

If any other way but bearing the cross, and dying to his own will, could have redeemed man from a fallen and corrupt state, Jesus would have taught it, and established it by his example.
