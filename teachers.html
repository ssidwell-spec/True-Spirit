A WARNING TO ALL Teachers of Children

Site Editor's Comment: We all are aware of today's problems with drugs, profanity, immorality, greed, dishonesty, crime, violence, etc. But this writing by George Fox reveals many aspects of our society that we assume to be correct and without question - cultural sacred cows. Fox shows how distant our behavior and standards are from godliness, and the missing values that are necessary to raise children so as to not be absorbed in their own vanity; he shows us that what we most highly value in the realm of education, conflicts with raising godly children. I publish this to open our eyes to how far we have devolved as a people. This is confirmed today by the voice of the Lord, the word within my heart, who has stated:

"As you can see, the state of mankind is not good. People of the world are taught vanity. There is no fear of God or common sense in the world. What is happening in the world is the result of the Lord being forgotten."

Fox's below writing is a vivid reality check on our society's lack of common sense, absence of the fear of God, and dedication to the pursuit of vanity.

A WARNING TO ALL Teachers of Children

Who are called School-Masters and School-Mistresses,
AND To Parents who send their Children to be taught by them.

That all School-Masters and School-Mistresses may train up children in the fear of God, and bring all children to that of God in themselves, with which they may come to fear him, and to depart from sin and evil. And how, that they may not be trained up in a customary teaching, and yet live in vanity, not being brought to the fear of God, they will be a grief to their parents in their old age.

Therefore, while the children are young, both parents, school-masters, and school-mistresses (so called) train the children up in the fear of God while they are young and tender, lest they be hardened.

This is to go into the world among all who are called school-masters and school-mistresses,

- By George Fox

YOU who are School-Masters and Mistresses (so called) and teachers of whatever sort, children ought to be taught the fear of  the Lord; and see that you teach them the fear of the Lord; the fear of the Lord God is to depart from sin, and to depart from evil, and here will they come to receive the wisdom of God, which is pure, not earthly, nor sensual, nor devilish; and being trained up in the fear of the Lord, to fear God, corrupt words and speeches, and evil words, and these they will be brought to depart from, which corrupts the good manners. So now, you who are School-Masters and School-Mistresses, and Teachers of children, and your children that you teach, are found in corrupt words, corrupt speaking, idle communications, and evil communications, and wantonness, and filthy pleasures, and filthy jesting, and filthy songs, and filthy rhymes; these actions are all among those who are out of the fear of God, teachers and scholars; and those who you teach being found in lying, and cursed speaking, and oaths, and mocking, and scoffing, and reproaching, and disdaining, and hating, and railing, and reviling, and slandering, and corrupt speeches, and backbiting, and maliciousness, and envy, and pride, and disdain, and in evil words, this shows that their manners are corrupt, though you may teach them all the scriptures, and they may learn all books by rote.

Therefore all you teachers of children, there is nothing worthy, while your children are found in these practices. If you are teachers having been taught the fear of the Lord, having departed from sin and evil, and received the wisdom of God, with which you should order and be ordered, then you will know how to teach children the fear of the Lord, with which you will know how to order them and to teach them the fear of God and bring them into the fear of God. This fear of God is to depart from sin and evil corrupt words, evil communication, wildness, wantonness and pleasures, foolish talking, and jesting, and vain sports, and pride, and ambition. To fear God is to be in lowness and meekness, free from  all abusive terms, carriage, posture, gesture, behavior, and words whatsoever that are contrary to truth; slandering, lying, mocking, reproaching, telling of tales, stories, jests, rhymes, fables, which feed the nature that is out of the fear of God. So that none of them backbite, calling evil names or nicknames to any people whatsoever. For evil words corrupt the good manners, and while those are lived in and acted, even if children are taught all books, and histories, and languages upon the earth, if they are found in the evil words, their manners are corrupt, and their language is not pure; the evil not being departed from, their lips perverse and polluted, their tongues uttering forth forward things, speaking perverse words. These are the cursed children, sporting in the day time, sporting themselves in wickedness which they are deceived by, though they may boast of their learning and languages, so learning these languages that puffs them up in the natural, and boasting of that, when they are void of the true knowledge and wisdom of God, and the true understanding, and that which should nurture man, and direct his paths. For a time their parents boast in these, and take much delight and glory in them, because they can speak many languages in their much learning; but being out of the fear of God, and found in the evil, out of God’s secrets, out of God’s counsel, out of his wisdom, and out of the true understanding, and the true knowledge, these at the last growing up in years, in pride, and in boisterousness, and unruliness, come to be a grief and sorrow to their parents, and run into the evil courses, having that which he has known naturally, in which he has corrupted himself, which shall perish in their corruption, for children ought all to be trained up in the nurture, and fear of God while they are young; for he that spares the rod does not love the child. All children ought to be taught to know the witness of God in themselves, which lets them see sin and transgression; and so that is it which must keep them from wildness, and wantonness, and corrupt words, and let them know when they speak and act them. Therefore all you School-Masters consider in what condition you yourselves stand, and then how you yourselves are taught, and then how you teach your children, and what condition you bring them up in, and in what condition you leave them; and what fruits follow them, and whether many were not better when they came to you, than when they left you. Again all you School-Mistresses, who teach children fashions according to the lust of ignorance, which fashions and lusts of the world are to pass away; and moreover teach your women to play on instruments and music of all kinds, and several tunes, and teaching them to dance, and catches [singing in the round], songs, and jests, which makes them lose modesty, and shamefacedness [shyness], but enlarges brazen-faces, and boldness, and impudence. This trains up people in that which draws from God, and keeps from out of the fear of God, training them up out of it, in wildness, and wantonness, and boldness; in defame and disdain, and envy of those who are out of their fashions. These characteristics are suitable for those who live in the lusts of the world, and have great habitations in them, who do not have a habitation in God, nor eternal life in them, and who live and abide in envy. All you who teach young men to fence, and keep fencing-schools, consider what you are doing in your very actions, whether you are not training them up in those actions that leads out of the fear of God, to glory in their strength, and to boast in it, in which it shall fall, and the carnal weapon with all its arts, and with all its hope and strength. Therefore come to the fear of God, to put on the armor of light, the armor of righteousness, and all come to Christ’s school and learn from him, that you may come to know the shield of faith, and breastplate of righteousness, the spiritual weapons. All you teachers of children and people, consider, for the Lord Jesus Christ is coming to look for fruits among you, and to teach himself. I am the Light of the world, and I enlighten every man who comes into the world, him by whom it was made, before it was made, glorified with the Father before the world began. Now all you who have come into the world, Christ having enlightened you all, with which Light you see sin and evil and know the Truth in the inward parts; which sin and evil must be departed from, before you come to receive the wisdom of God, which if you act contrary to the Light (which Christ has enlightened you with so that through the Light you may believe) and go from it into evil communications that corrupt the good manners; as you abide in the Light, which Christ has enlightened you with, you will know the good manners and teach children the same, which brings them to depart from evil words which corrupt them; so that children might come to the Light, and hearken to that which lets them see their transgression, and makes manifest their sin; the light which Christ enlightens every man that comes into the world with, which is in them, which will let them see their evil words, and sin, and evil. Evil communications, which must be departed from, for if not, that corrupts the good manners; and the Light will let you see all wantonness, and pleasures, foolish talking, and jestings must be departed from; and it will teach you soberness, and watchfulness, and the fear of God, and to be grave and temperate, and to give good words, and to know good words, and the pure communications, and the good manners, which is honorable and of good report; and it will bring you to know the adorning, which is a meek and a quiet spirit, which is with the Lord of great price, and this light (which every one of you are enlightened with, who have come into the world, which comes from the Father of light. It will bring you from the lust of the eye, [Mark, from the lusts of the eye] and the lusts of the flesh, and the pride of life, and the costly attire, and the costly array, or of wearing gold or silver, and let you know and see these things, which are not of the Father, but of the World. The light that comes from the Father, which if you all love, the light which you are enlightened withal, it will teach you in private when you are by yourselves, and when you are among company, (both children and Teachers); teaching you to forsake that which the Light makes manifest, which if you do not, that Light will be your condemnation, said Christ. So I speak to the Light in you all, (and remember that you are all warned in your lifetime), which is my witness, and your condemnation, if you hate it.

George Fox

Do not lose your zeal for God; come to know that which quenches your zeal for the Lord, in which [zeal] the life [of God] preserves; whatever is feigned and a will worship, ( pleasing the will of people), and the feigned flattery, quenches the zeal, and brings in another clothing. Whoever is clothed with zeal, have put off that clothing, for that is worn in the night; there [in darkness] is a self pleasing of men for self-ends and for gain, a flattery to the face, and then a speaking evil behind the back when the men or the women are gone; and this is deceit, treachery, falsehood among people, dissimulation, and hypocrisy. Therefore deny selfishness in all things, for out of that ground comes the men pleasers, which [selfishness] covers the principle of God that should lead to please God and to please men in God, as men deny themselves, curiosity in speech, and quick in knowledge, in the earthly, selfish principle. Here lies the craft, and deceit, and quick in the understanding [fleshly] that comes to naught, and the wisdom that is earthly, for the earth, and for the self, which is to be confounded before it comes into God's wisdom; and that will say anything for self ends. Take heed of that principle, and take heed of flattery, but be upright, single and true. Live in that [zeal for God], and truth in all charging of the creature, in all speaking to your neighbor, in all dealings; and in such you will feel the blessing of the Lord God's arm with you. The feigned, fleshly, selfish hypocrites, who live in a hypocritical profession, still in the earthly selfish principle, are all whose religion stinks, and in the eyes of God is an abomination; and because of this God's plagues comes upon such people, and crosses their wills and their expectations; and it is right and just so to do.

You who have been fattened with the creature, and have kicked against the just, and have called upon God with your lips, and you pray evening and morning, and you are pretending to serve him in your sermons, and expressions, and you have read the Prophets’ words the Apostles’ words, and my son’s words, and when you have done, and while you are doing, your hearts are roving after your possessions, as you buy and sell the creatures; and here you call upon me with your lips, and your hearts are far off. Such sacrifices are not to be accepted; your prayers and your reading Prophets’ and Apostles’ words and my Son’s words, while not being guided with my spirit, your hypocrisy will manifest itself, your rash words, and your headiness, and your wildness, shame your profession in your streets, and manifests that you only serve him with your lips. Your old men have grown drunken in the flesh, and foolish, and selfish, and perverse, and willful, and stubborn, and not grave and temperate; and your old women are tattlers and too full of tongue, and given to talk, and so both old men and old women grieve my spirit, for both are given to vanity, and the lust, and fashions of this world. They are vain talkers, in earthliness and selfishness; they have been brutish against my truth, and you have caused my name to be evil spoken of. Your young men and maidens are light, and vain, and wild, and heady, and this has dishonored me, though they profess my name. So that both old men and young men, and old women and young women, have both dishonored their profession; young men and young women have both been trained up without my fear, so that they are as wild as Esau's colts were, and the young women are as wild as wild heifers, and the old men are as far as the Bulls of Bashan. Your old men and old women have spoiled your children; you have served the desires of your minds, and served the desires of your children’s minds, by which they are given to vanity, following the fashions and the lust of ignorance. The pride of life is among you in a high manner, the lust of the eye is among you exceedingly full, and the lust of the flesh which is not of me; but my spirit has been grieved, which should have mortified this, which should have led you to have worshipped me in my spirit; then you would have known the Prophets’ words, and the Apostles’ words, and my Son’s words; but unless you all come to feel my spirit, and that which is manifest in you, which God has shown to you, to lead your minds to God, and to circumcise you. Unless you feel my Spirit do this, you must go down into the pit with all the uncircumcised, though you profess the Prophets,’ Apostles,’ and my Son’s words.

Your eyes have been evil, and your ways have not been right; you have walked without my fear, and have not given me the honor, but taken it to yourselves. And now, (says God), left in it, your teachers have been fleshly, big, up-swollen in the flesh, and they have had my Prophets’words, my Son’s words, but they and you have been out of my Son's life, and the Apostles' life; now I will measure your ways with that, and measure your lives, and measure your fruits, and measure your actions, and deeds, and measure your assemblies, who have assembled together, but not by me, and without my Spirit. I abhor your fasts, and your assemblies; they stink, while your are covetous and proud, and hard-hearted; the fruits of this is declared, and not tenderness. You would destroy the meek of the earth; and loftiness is seen among you in the extreme, and you have gloried in my creatures, more then in me the Creator, and surfeited yourselves with my creatures, which burdens and bondages the creature with your unreasonableness. Yet you will come before me with your lips, pretending to pray to me, and serve me, and call upon me. The deep ditch has swallowed up many, and so the pit; vanity, lust, and carelessness drown many and destroys many. I am pressed down with your unrighteousness, with your iniquities, and with your bondage of corruption. You have lived out of my fear, so my wisdom and my secrets are hidden from you, and you have been presumptuous, boasting of my Prophets,' Apostles,' and Son's words; but have been one with your forefathers who put him [Christ] to death. But you shall all give an account to me; I will bring you all to judgment. Abundance of feigned love has been among you. When I have opened anything to you, you have quenched my Prophet; the fleshly nature has boasted in you, of the words in the flesh, and so you have gone in Korah's and Balaam's way into the earth. Again you have gathered churches, and set up churches of your own making. You do not have my power nor the spirit that my Apostles and Prophets were in; therefore I break you into sects, and tread you into heaps; and I break all of you who trust in man, said the Lord, the Creator of all things, that you may all come to feel my Spirit and that to lead you into all truth, for they are my Sons who are led by my Spirit.

And all your vain contentions, and arguments, and vain disputes about my Prophets,' Apostles,' and Son's words, and out of the life, you will I judge, your fruits have manifested confusion which I have not been the author of, said the Lord. But you have transgressed my Spirit, and so brought forth confusion. Darkness has covered you, mists and darkness, so a whirlwind is among you, a day of tempests and storms. What your Teachers have sown, (who never heard my voice, and do not have my Power and Spirit, which my Prophets and Apostles were in, but have gotten my Prophets,' Apostles,' and Son's words, and put off their life), they have sown to the flesh, they may reap corruption, they are to reap it, their field is large, every one of their words shall be their burden, who have spoken in a pretence from me, and have not heard my word nor my voice, nor are in my Spirit; such sow vanity, and cause my people to err by your lies and by your lightness; and behold, I am against such said the Lord God almighty; you are tinkling cymbals (out of my love) that pretend high things, you are sounding brass; they sound like brass, (out of my love); there is hay, wood, and stubble brought forth; which when it is touched with the fire, it burns. This is from the tried cornerstone, that is elect and precious, that abides and remains forever, which is and was before the world was, and yet was manifested in the world. The world has gotten the words that speak of it, and has declared the generation of it, which the Prophets and Apostles had. So you are out of my Prophets' life, you teachers and professors of the world, who have my Prophets' and Apostles' words, which have declared the generation of the tried stone, the cornerstone, but are out of the life; and this stone is set upon all your heads, and on him whom it falls, it shall grind him to powder. This is my word to you all said the Lord.

And all you who are making noises about Christ, while living on top of the life and witness, who wear the old garments of briars and thorns, which covers the Light; the just will go over you all, and goes, and reaches over you all, and the power is over you all, and fathoms, and God is exalted over you all, and will burn up your briars and thorns. All people, cease from talking of God and Christ with the light minds, (who is not of the world), for this is only from your fleshly knowledge, and light minds, which is the manifestation of your erring from my Spirit, that you may know Christ to reign [in you], who now reigns, and is come to reign over the world, who is the end of his Prophets, who was before the world was made, who now is come to reign and rule the nations with a rod of iron; glory in the highest.

George Fox

Advice to Children: Excerpt from Francis Howgill's letter to his daughter, as he was dying in prison.

My counsel unto you is that you remember your Creator in the days of your youth, and fear the Lord in your youth, and learn to know him and serve him all your days. First seek the kingdom of God and the righteousness thereof; it is not far from you; it is within you. It consists in life and power, and it stands in righteousness, truth, and equity; justice, mercy, long-suffering, patience, love, light, and holiness, this is the being and center thereof. Therefore don't seek God in this place or that place, without you, in this or that outward observation, for many seek there and never find it; but seek and you shall find, wait and you shall receive.

If you inquire, in what must I seek? And in what must I wait? And how must I seek? I inform you, that you must silence all your own thoughts, and you must turn your mind to what is pure, and holy, and good within yourself and seek and wait in that, in the light of Jesus Christ, by which you are enlightened, which shows you when you do evil, and checks and reproves you. Take heed to that, and it will show your evil inclinations and thoughts; and as you love it, it will destroy them, and preserve you for the time to come out of evil. For though you are born into the world a reasonable creature, yet you must be born again, and be made a new creature, or else you can not enter into God's kingdom. You must know the seed of the kingdom in yourself, of which you must be born and formed again into God's image. I have told you God has sown it in you, a grain of it, a measure of it, a portion of it, a measure of light and truth, of righteousness and holiness; keep your thoughts focused to that, and love it, and you will feel the heavenly Father working in you, and forming you to life through Jesus Christ who has enlightened you. Doing this, you will feel the power of the Lord strengthening you, from your small beginning, and making you grow in the immortal seed of his kingdom, while outgrowing and overgrowing all evil. So that you will daily die to evil, and have no pleasure in it; but rather your pleasure will be in the Lord, and in his goodness and virtue shed abroad in your heart, which you will taste and feel within, and in which you will have joy and comfort.

Love the Lord with your heart and soul, even him that made you, and gave you your life, and all things in heaven and earth. Be still and wait for the knowledge of him in yourself. He is not far from you, but near you, and to all that call upon him in an upright heart. Seek the counsel of your dear mother; she will inform you; she knows him, and the way to life and peace; and listen to her instructions.

God is a spirit, of light, and life, and power, that searches the heart, and shows you when you do, or think, or speak evil, and shows man or woman their thoughts. What shows the evil, is good; and what shows a lie, is truth; this is within, take heed to it. This is called God's Spirit in the Scriptures; believe in it, love it, and it will quicken your heart to goodness, and it will subject the evil. Here is your teacher near you; love it, and if you act contrary, it will condemn you. Therefore take heed unto this spirit of truth, and it will enlighten and enliven you, and will open your understanding, and give you to know what God is, and to do what is good and acceptable in his sight; this spirit never errs, but leads out of all error into all truth.

O Abigail, believe my words! These are the words of God, and Truth. Be sober-minded in your youth, and wait on the Lord within; listen unto him. God is light immortal, life immortal, truth immortal, an everlasting eternal Spirit. He speaks spiritually and invisibly within the hearts and consciences of men and women. Hear what he speaks, and obey his voice, and your soul shall live; fear to offend him, or sin against him, for the wages of sin are death. Therefore prize his love in your young and tender years, and do read the scriptures and Friends' books, and take heed to what you read to obey it, as far as you understand; and pray often unto the Lord, that he will give you his knowledge, and open your understanding in the things of his kingdom; search your heart often with the light of Christ in you; manifest and bring your deeds to it, that they may be tried. Examine yourself how the case stands between the Lord and you; and if you see yourself wrong, humble yourself and be sorry, and turn unto him, and he will show you mercy. And take heed for the time to come, that you do not repeat the same evil again. Keep your heart clean, and watch against the evil in yourself by the light which shows the evil; in which light there is power, and thereby you have power to overcome all evil.

And, dear child, mind not the pleasures of sin, which are but for a moment, and the end is misery; but keep low and cross your will and affections, so your mind will have no pleasure in the evil, but in good; and you will feel the immortal seed springing up in you, which God's peace and love is to.

Oh Abigail! These are great and weighty things, not to be slighted. Seek the company of the people who fear the Lord, worship him in spirit and truth, and lead a holy and blameless life and conversation; do not deny these people, but love them, and suffer with them. Take heed that you don't follow the hireling teachers and preachers, who preach for gain and money, and do not abide Christ's doctrine. Don't believe them, don't heed them, they do people no good. But you will see them yourself. They have an outside show of godliness sometimes, but deny the power of God and true holiness; remember, I who have had perfect knowledge of them, have told you. But be sure that you let nothing separate your love from God and his people. His people were ever hated and belied, and persecuted, and evil-spoken of, always by bad and evil, loose people. His people are those who keep his law, and obey Christ's voice, and lead a holy life; these are God's people, and his love, and peace, and blessing, is with them. Love and associate with his people as you grow as a natural branch, (up among them), of the living vine, and continue all your days in obedience unto God's will, and you will feel joy and love in your heart; which you should desire above all things, and you will attain and obtain everlasting peace, which the Lord grants unto you, according to the riches of his mercy and love, which endure forever and ever. Amen.

 

THE END
