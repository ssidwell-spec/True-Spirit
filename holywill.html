Teach me to do your will for you are my God; your spirit is good; lead me into the land of uprightness.
Psa 143:10

The will of our glorious God is for us to know Him, to seek Him as our first priority, and be drawn out of darkness by our love for Him. To give up living to our own will and die to self. To give to others as if to Him. To believe in the Light which is Him, and wait quietly for Him who is within each of us, so He can re-create us to be pure as He is pure, and bring us to see Him in his glory. To adore Him forever, and to be his sons, in total union with Him —so that all of our judgments, words, and actions are controlled by the promptings of God —His will, not our will.

To seek Him as our first priority

But seek first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things will be added to you. Mat 6:33

To seek God, find him, and be united with him is the single and sole purpose of life. He [God] has made from one blood all nations of men to dwell on all the face of the earth, and has determined the allotted times and the boundaries of their habitation; so that they should seek the Lord, and perhaps might feel after Him and find Him, though He is not far from every one of us. For in Him we live and move and have our being. One God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all. Acts 17:26-28, Eph 4:6

Keep on seeking and you will find. Mat 7:7

If ...you will seek the LORD your God, you will find Him if you seek Him with all your heart and with all your soul. Deu 4:2

This is the prayer of truth: it is “worshiping God in spirit and truth.” because here we come to know the Spirit to help our infirmities, and make intercession for us; and being thus influenced by the pure spirit of God, we are drawn forth and freed from our own carnal and corrupt manner of praying [to that of quiet waiting]. He died on the cross to show us how to die on our spiritual cross.
He is the pattern Son, to show us how to die spiritually to self.
And the cross to us is nothing compared to his. It is easy and light.
When we sit and wait on Him and watch for at least an hour, our will is to get up and do something else.
When He shows us our condition, our will is to run or deny that we are that bad.
When He commands to give up doing certain things, our will is to keep doing them.
So when we do the opposite of our will and repent, we are on the cross, dying to self.
This is the process of repentance.
Here we can be led by Him, cleansed by Him, taught by Him, and come to Know Him.
Our death is a spiritual death.
To share the cross with Christ, however light ours is.
To share death with Christ, however easy ours is.
To share the resurrection with Christ, both to his glory.
We see the effects of the changes in us-in breathless awe and loving thanksgiving,
and we begin to understand why he came and what he is doing.

For Jesus said: "unless you repent, all of you will also perish." Luke 13:3,5
