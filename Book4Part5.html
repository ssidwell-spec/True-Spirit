THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN THE WORLD'S RELATIONSHIP,

Which stands in Strife, Malice, and Wickedness, and the Saints' Relationship, which stands in Love, Life, Peace and Joy in the Holy Ghost.

—By G. F.

The relationship, which stands in destroying,
is denied by the children of the light.
All such who live out of light, and out of it walk,
who wear the old garment, whose mind is corrupt,
and have a wisdom, but with it know not God,
and have a knowledge, and with it corrupt themselves,
and have a humility, but it is false,
and have a worship, but in the will of the carnal mind,
and have a righteousness, but in the self,
and speak the high words, but it is in hypocrisy,
and have a liberty, but in the flesh,
where the spirit is in prison.
All such in Cain's way have gone, and have not a habitation in God,
whose affections are below in the earth, and serve the lusts and the pleasures,
whose conduct is among the wicked,
and there is the world's relationship where strife lodges,
and the debate and deceit, and disdain,
and setting up that which God will throw down.
Now while this is standing, which is contrary to the light,
those who walk in the light and are in it, deny those,
and see that which leads nature out of its course,
and what sets the whole course of nature on fire;
and there the whole world is in confusion,
and all the tongues are unbridled, where the power is not over them,
while that reigns which leads nature out of its course.
Of such, his religion is vain, and that relationship stands out of truth;
but all those who are turned to the light, see with the light,
(which reveals what leads nature out of  its course),
the marriage that is honorable,
and what sets the whole course of nature on fire,
and what leads nature into course,
where the pure relationship and the entrance of it is received,
where the self and its righteousness is denied,
and the righteousness of Christ received;
where the worship of the will is denied,
and the worship of God in spirit and truth received;
where self-righteousness is denied,
and Christ the righteousness of God received,
and the former conduct put off,
and the conduct in heaven witnessed,
the affections below changed,
and the affections from above are come to.

Here is the pure religion known without guile, who are out of Cain's way,
where the habitation in God is witnessed and seen,
where the just live by his faith, and honor in the Lord known,
and the true humility learned of Christ,
who is not of the world, though it was made by him,
who lights every man that comes into the world,
who is man's way out of it;
which light is man's condemnation, if he does not believe in it;
and the relationship which is in the world (who hate the light) is in the lust,
where the perverseness is in the love of the world,
where the envy is, where the invention is,
selfishness, crossness, perverseness, backbiting, maliciousness, filled with  unrighteousness,
yet they have a love, yet they have a relationship.
Now the light which comes from the beloved Son of God,
where the love of God is received, the world's relationship is denied,
for the relationship of the world destroys and devours,
but the children of the light, which are in the relationship out of the world,
live in peace, unity, and fellowship, and comfort enjoyed in the Holy Ghost;
so all you that profess relationship in words, when envy lodges in your hearts against one another,
and yet flatter one another to your faces, and backbite one another behind the back,
such relationships we deny, and have a witness in your consciences against it.

And all that profess a relationship one to another,
and love and friendship with the tongue, and in heart deceive one another,
and cozen and cheat one another, and lie, and dissemble one with another,
all such relationships we deny, and have a witness in your conscience against it.
Those who profess religion, or worship, or humility, righteousness,
and the scripture, Christ and the prophets' words,
and live out of their life, and live in envy and malice,
and yet profess you have a relationship to Christ, and God, whom you have not seen,
and profess you love him, but do not love those whom you have seen,
such relationships we do deny.
All who cry up your experiences, high notions, manifestations,
as you call them, dispensations as you call them,
and live in pride, strife, and envy, debates, derision, scorns, loftiness, and high-mindedness,
you are out of the light, and out of the way,
in corners, and out of the pure relationship.
We have a witness in your conscience against those things,
which lets you see what you possess,
and will let you profess no more if you listen to those things ,
which light turns from the world and the relationships therein,
for all who do not believe in the light, have not come to it.
They have a love, but it is in the world,
and a relationship, but it is in the world, which is enmity [enemy] to God.
So that relationship which is enmity, is denied with the light,
that love which is enmity, is denied with the light,
which leads to the pure relationship, which envies not,
where the destroyer and devourer is rebuked.

George Fox

The faith of such is no better than the faith of the Pharisees,
who only believe of a Christ, because the scriptures declare of him,
but the faith of them who dwell in the life and power of God,
and follow the Lamb wherever he goes,
is that faith which overcomes the world.

To believe only of a Christ because the scriptures declare of him, is to believe as the Pharisees believed, who were the persecutors and murderers of the just, but to believe in the light, the life that gave forth the scriptures is seen, and Christ is believed in, and here he has the witness in himself, and comes to be a child of light, and born of God, which birth overcomes the world. Now, said Christ, they hate the light, because their deeds are evil, and will not bring their deeds to it, because the light will reprove them, and this is the condemnation, the light, said Christ. Now it appears who hate the light: priests, teachers, people, young and old hate the light, and will not bring their deeds to the light, because the light will reprove them. Neither will they allow others that walk in the light, and love the light, and bring their deeds to the light, to show them their deeds with the light, and reprove them with the light, because they hate the light, and those who walk in it, and cast them into prison, and so are stumbling at the light all sorts of people, [mark,] it is the light that they hate, said Christ, they hate the light which reproves their evil deeds.

This light is that which the world and its teachers cannot endure to hear talk of, but hate it, and those who walk in the light. It is the light that is so much stumbled at, because their deeds are evil, and they walk in darkness, there is a proof; for those who walk in the light stumble not, but come to receive the light of life. Here Christ's words are fulfilled among them that hate the light, and will not bring their deeds to the light, because the light will reprove them. For that which makes manifest is light; so that which makes manifest they cannot endure, but would be in the dark, and keep their deeds in the dark as it is manifest. Christ said the devil shall cast some of you into prison; he is out of the light, out of the truth, and a liar, and a murderer from the beginning, which now is seen with the light, and trampled upon. All who do not own the light, which comes from the word [within you heart and mouth], are the wicked seeds-men, sowing abroad for tares; there lies, the evil reports. Those who are gone from the light, that comes from the word of truth, are given up to believe lies and reports, and may cry up darkness.  If you hate the light, what do you have to preach, other than darkness; if you turn from the light, pitiful preaching results, which hates the light, which manifests all the works of darkness.

George Fox

TO THE PEOPLE OF UXBRIDGE

Friends,—Your conditions is grieved and lamented over, your want of the life of God, and barrenness of his power among you, whose lean souls are seen, whose barrenness, and barren womb appears, who thirst little after the living God, whose wildness, wantonness, and negligence of the everlasting salvation appears; yet full, like Sodom and Gomorrah, being full of that which is wicked and profane; the streets of your town will manifest it. O, people, where is your salvation, and what assurance have you of that, and of your eternal inheritance, where nothing that does defile enters? O, are you come to examine and see whether Christ is in you, or not, that wickedness, profaneness, unrighteousness, ungodliness, filthiness might go out of you? For if Christ is found within you, the strong man goes out that has kept the house, and defiled the heart, and the body, and the mind, and that imprisoned the soul; if Christ is felt within, there is the soul's bishop. O, people, I am grieved with your abominations, and filthiness, and unsavoriness, and untowardness, and your unsubjected state to the life and power of God. If you do not repent, and your minds are not changed to serve the living God in spirit, which mortifies sin and corruption, the earth will vomit you out, who have cumbered it, and destroyed the creatures of God on your filthy ungodly lusts, and that you have served and pleasured. O, I am weary of mine adversaries, said the Lord, I am weary of your abominations, you have loaded the just, and pressed it as a cart with sheaves, your hearts are gross and fat; with your tongues you have talked of God, but your hearts have been far off, after your own ends: your younger children, boys and people, are full of lightness, wildness, folly, and vanity, and envy, and mocking, scorn and derision, (which is not a good savor in your streets), stubbornness, and untowardness, and profaneness, and wildness, more like Esau's and Ishmael's stock, than Isaac's and Jacob's. Your old men are not sober, nor solid, nor grave, and so they are not good patterns to the younger; so you are all more like heathens than christians, empty of the good, full of the forgetfulness of God, and out of his fear, as though there was no God, as though you had never heard talk of God, nor scripture, nor Christ; but this saying is fulfilled, the teachers have not profited the people at all, Jer 23. But like people, like priest. But what a teacher is he that must serve this people? Who must be the prophet, or the priest, that must fit such a people? Surely he must be such a one who teaches for filthy lucre, and bears rules by his means, that will divine for money, that will preach peace to them, while they put into his mouth, such as mind earthly things, who serves not the Lord Jesus Christ, but their own bellies, that has such fruits in the streets. Butthose who seek for their gain from their quarter, and the price, never lead people to the Lord, but lead them into the earth, and to be like themselves, and they live like beasts and swine, as you may read. Jer 5, Mic 3, Eze 34, Isa 56, Jer 23, Rom 13, Mat 12, whose fruits declare it.

And away with the profession of God with your lips, while your hearts are afar off; and away with your making a trade of the scriptures, Christ's and the prophets' and the apostles' words, for which many suffered death in giving them forth. Away with your lukewarmness, and away with your carnal formality, and away with your doctrines and traditions of men, which perish in the using of them. Away with your singing of David's quakings, and tremblings, and fasts in meter, and instead, come to sing in the spirit. Away with your hour-glass-preaching, and hypocritical praying, and your old mass-house-worship, which is called steeple-house or church, and come to the church in God. 1 Thes 1, and to worship God in spirit, John 4, and the Lord is come to teach his people himself, by his spirit, and to bring them off from all the world's teachers, and churches, and ways, to Christ, the way to himself, and he does enlighten everyone that comes into the world, that all through the light might believe; and everyone believing in the light, which has enlightened them, they shall become the children of the light; but those who hate the light that does enlighten them, they hate Christ, and that condemns them: and that is the light that lets the drunkard see he should not be drunk, and the curser that he should not curse, nor swear, and the thief, that he should not steal, and that the cozener should not cozen, nor cheat, nor wrong, nor defraud; and lets the profane see he should not profane; and lets the envious man see he should not envy, nor fight, nor quarrel, nor lie, nor commit adultery; that is the light which discovers that: not to look after a woman to lust after her, that is the light that said so. If you love the light, it will let you see all your evil thoughts, words, and actions, which be wrought out of God, and turn you from them; and coming into the light, your works will be wrought in God, and your words will be from him, and so good, that is in everyone of your bosoms that will judge you, and condemn you acting contrary to it, and reprove you; but if you consider it, and hearken to it, it will turn your minds to the Lord God; and if you hate it, it will be your condemnation. Therefore, now while you have time, consider and prize it, for this is the day of your visitation, and salvation proffered to you. With the light which you see your sin, which Christ Jesus has enlightened you withal, with the same you may see your salvation, Christ Jesus, from whence it comes, to save you from your sin. And people, that which may be known of God is made manifest within you, which God has showed unto you, that when you do the thing which is not convenient, not righteous, but worthy of death, by that of God in you, you can know; and while your minds are reprobate from that, you are all strangers from the life of God, and the covenant of promise. And friends, the light you must feel in your own hearts, to give you the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Christ, 2 Cor 4, and all the children of the Lord, shall be taught of the Lord, Isa 54, and you must come to know the anointing in you, to teach you, 1 John 2, and the kingdom of heaven you must know within you, Luke 17, and that is it which never consents to sin. And you must come to know the new covenant, the law in your minds, and in your hearts, by which you need not say to one another, know the Lord, but that all might come to know him, from the greatest to the least, that witness the law of God put and written in your minds and hearts, that you may come to witness the Lord is your God, and you his people. Repent, for fear that you come to say you had time, when it is passed; so from a lover of your souls, and your eternal good.

George Fox

Come, away with your self-righteousness, and your feigned humility, and your will-worship, and your carnal security, and take heed of drunkenness, and filthiness, and profaneness, and scoffing, mocking, scorning, and derision, for such who live in such things, and act such things, shall not inherit the kingdom of God; and away with that you call your sacraments, and your sprinkling infants, and come to Christ the substance, and come to the spirit, that you may be baptized into one body, that you may come to possess the things the scriptures speak of.

The very mist of the air is among you, and so are so light, and vain, which are the prince of the air's fruits, and the priest's who has long preached for his mouth; but if you put not into his mouth, see if he turn not against you, and you shall not keep him long; so look unto Christ Jesus to teach you, who said, learn of me, who is the way to the Father, who has enlightened you, that with the light you may see him, who is the bread to feed upon; but if you hate it, then you feed upon husks.

This is to be read among the people of Uxbridge.

A WORD IN THE BEHALF OF THE KING,

That he may see who they are that honor all Men, and love the Brotherhood, that fear God, and honor the King; according as it is written in the Scriptures of Truth. 1 Pet 2:17.

— By George Fox

Let all people consider what they do, and what they say, and practice; who say they are christians (and go under that name) and bring Peter's words,1 Pet 2:17, Honor all men, [Mark,] that is a large word, for if they honored all men, then they would not hurt any man, but love all men, and have all men in esteem; and if they esteemed a man, they would never hurt him; and if they esteemed all men, and honored them, then they would hurt no man. So if they only practiced what they say, there would be no strife in Christendom; nor would there be strife with any men, nor hatred of any man, nor violence, nor striking any man -  for he honored him, and had him in esteem, and he that honored him, loved him.

Now he that does not honor all men, nor does not love all men, nor has not all men in esteem, (though he may say these words of the apostle, yet he is out of the doctrine of the apostle, and practice) he is in the nature of a Jew, or a heathen, that hates, and dishonors, slanders, backbites, and envies, and does violence to men, does not honor them, nor has them not in esteem, nor does not love them. So look what derision, scorn, maliciousness, and what envy men live in, abusing one another, and wronging one another, yet would not these be called christians? do these honor all men? Do these love all men, even their enemies? Have these all men in esteem, that will strike, and fight, and shed men's blood? Are these such as keep Christ's commands, who said, “You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your mind, with all your soul, and with all your strength, and you shall love your neighbor as yourself.” Luke 10:27, Again he said, “All things whatsoever you would that men should do unto you, do you the same unto them, for that is the law and the prophets.” Luke 6:31. Now he that loves his neighbor as himself, he will not hurt him; and he that wrongs or deceives his neighbor, or strikes, or abuses, or sheds another man's blood, he would not have the same done unto himself by another; and so such an one is out of Christ's doctrine, and a transgressor of the law and the prophets.

Now is it not so, that many esteem clothes and riches, and so admire men's persons because of advantage. Thus they have become respecters of men's persons, and do not honor all men, but disesteem them that have not fine clothes, and gay apparel, and riches, and esteem them that have; and so honor the one, and dishonor the other; now this is respecting of persons, and not to honor all men, as the apostle James in 2:12 speaks of, who said, “My brethren have not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory with respect of persons. For if there come a man with a gold ring, and goodly apparel, and there come in also a poor man in vile raiment, and you have respect to him that wears the gay clothing, and say unto him, sit you here in a good place, and say to the poor man stand you there, or sit here under my foot-stool.” Now this is being the respecter of persons, who has not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, and not the one who honors of all men, and so there is the sect maker; but who honors all men, they are no sect makers, but are out of them all.

The second thing is this, “Love the brotherhood, and love enemies;” now here is the two things, to love enemies, it is not to kill them, and to destroy them, but to overcome them with the good; now enemies are sons of Adam, in whom the enmity is, and the sins of the flesh, who be strangers from the life of God, and the covenant of promise and sonship, in which is the brotherhood.

Those that are in the love of God, (which is to the world), would have all others to be saved, and to know their mind and their salvation. Now if they would have all men to be saved, they would not have them destroyed, neither destroy them. Even if they are hypocrites, or blasphemers, or dissenters, or heretics, or fanatics, let them be what they will; he would have them to be saved. The patience and long suffering of God is great salvation. Paul and David, who were great transgressors, witnessed this salvation. But the impatient and envious, (which are the tares), they destroy the saints, and pluck up the wheat for tares, as blasphemers and heretics. But these have not the spirit of Christ guiding of them, and such cannot love enemies, for those who hate enemies, are out of the commands of Christ, and out of his spirit, and mind, who persecute about religion, and church worship, but do not love enemies; neither are they in brotherly fellowship, nor in the spirit and power of God, that goes over all transgression, that was before it was. So those who hate enemies have not all men in esteem, (mark, all men), these are not in brotherly fellowship, nor do not love brotherhood, but are respecters of persons, that do not honor all men, and love enemies; but are in hatred, and envy, and fury, and rage, and madness, (not in the love and meekness), and that is the fanatic spirit, that does not love enemies.

Thirdly, “Fear God and honor the king;” so mark, those who fear God, honor the king; and to fear God is to depart from sin, and evil, (and that keeps the heart clean) which is the beginning of wisdom. Now such as do not fear God, do not honor the king; and such as do not depart from sin and evil, do not fear God, and they do not honor the king.

It may be they may talk of him with the lips, and bow the knee, put off the hat, etc. But that is not the honor; for those who live not in the fear of God, do not honor the king. Drunkards, and swearers, and cursers, you fear not God, therefore you honor not the king; for the scriptures said, “you shall not swear nor curse,” as Paul said, to the Romans , and as Christ said, Mat 5:32-37 and James said, James 5:12. And all you who live in adultery, fornication, uncleanness, idolatry, whoredom, lasciviousness, effeminateness, pleasures, gaming, sporting, pastime, which stirs up vain and idle words, (for which you must give an account) and vain actions, and taking God's name in vain, you fear not God that do so; therefore you honor not the king, but do dishonor him, and this nation, and his government, and his kingdom; “for righteousness exalts a nation, but sin is a reproach to any people,” said Solomon; and, said the apostle, “this know you, that no whoremonger, nor unclean person, or covetous man, who is an idolater has any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ, or of God.” Eph 5:5-6.

And all you cozeners, cheaters, defrauders, dissemblers, liars, backbiters, slanderers, false accusers, envious, and malicious persons, murderers, plotters, fighters, quarrelers, brawlers, scolders, tumultuous ones, rioters, ambitious, proud, heady, high-minded, vain-glorious, perverse, wicked, and willful men, you live not in God's fear, therefore you cannot honor the king; for they who honor the king, must fear God, as you may read, 1 Pet 2:17. All reproachers, scoffers, scorners, despisers, and mockers, and such as give nicknames to one another, and foolish jesters, and vain, and corrupt, and filthy talkers, who vomit out your own shame by your corrupt communication, you do not fear God; therefore you do not honor the king in your hearts. All of you must come to the witness of God in yourselves, which lets you see sin and evil, and then depart from it. The fear of God is to depart from sin and evil, and then you come to honor the king as you live in God's fear, and there is no other way.

All you who are earthly-minded, and live in oppression, extortion, grinding the face of the poor, in covetousness, which is idolatry, and rebellion against God, which is as the sin of witchcraft, and stubbornness, which is iniquity, and say you shall have peace, though you walk in the stubbornness of your hearts, and regard not the Lord; you do not fear God, neither do you honor the king.

All you insober, intemperate, immodest, ungracious, who choose vice rather than virtue and piety, you fear not God, but cast his law and his fear behind your backs; therefore you honor not the king, for you are to be in the fear of God, before you honor the king.

So never profess that you honor the king, who do not live in the fear of God; and never profess that you are fearing God, and honoring of the king, while you are cursing, swearing, damning, roaring, drinking healths, and spoiling, and misspending, consuming, and destroying the creatures, and drinking in, and vomiting up again. All that be there (and are so doing), fear not God, and so they do not honor the king, (this is not for the king's health, nor this is not for the king's honor), but as I said before, such dishonor him.

All that follow the vain fashions of this world, that live in the lusts of the flesh, the lust of the eye, and the pride of life, which is not of God the Father, but of the world, these fear not God, so they honor not the king.

And all thieves, robbers, man-slayers, plotters to destroy men's persons, lives and estates, to bring men into poverty, and to undo one another; these live not in the fear of God, which is to love enemies, and to do good for evil; and these honor not all men, these love not the brotherhood, these cannot honor the king, that live not in God's fear.

All those who make discord, and strife, and dissension among people by lies, and false reports, and dissemble to get gain, and lay snares for the righteous, and betray the innocent to get gain and advantage to themselves, by telling lies of some to others, these fear not God, these do not honor the king.

And all who envy one another about their fashions, and diet, because they have not means to maintain it as high as others, though they would be in the same fashion, and live as high as others, but their estates will not maintain it, and therefore they envy all others who have estates, and can maintain it; and likewise those who have estates to maintain their fashions, etc. They envy others that would be there, and do as they do, but have not wherewithal to maintain it; and so these do not esteem all men, nor love brotherhood, nor fear God, nor honor the king, that have not departed from these evils; for those who go into evil, and live in evil, or in any manner of sin and wickedness, do not fear God, but they fear God, that depart from sin and evil, and such are those who honor all men, and love the brotherhood, who depart from sin, and these are those who honor the king.

But now, cursers, swearers, covenant-breakers, perjured persons, who will swear, and break oaths, and forswear, make covenants, and vows, and promises, and protestations, and leagues, and break them, such fear not God, nor honor the king.

And such as will fight, and kill, and destroy for a morsel of bread, or a mess of pottage, are profane, as Esau was. These are men without natural affection, lovers of themselves, and lovers of pleasures more than lovers of God, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, truce-breakers, incontinent, fierce, despisers of them that are good, traitors, and men of corrupt minds, and to every good work are reprobates. These do not fear God, neither can these honor the king, nor the kingdom, nor nation wherein they live.

All that follow gluttony, excess and riot, who eat and drink, and rise up to play, feasts, banquets, stage-plays, bear-baitings, cock-fights, horse-races, hunting, hawking, dancing, carousing, wantonness, spending their times after the lusts and pleasures of the world, which perish with the using; these fear not God, these are not like to honor the king, that destroy the creatures upon their lusts, and vain pleasures.

Therefore tremble before the Lord God, you that do not fear his name, but live in sin, and wickedness, enviousness, maliciousness, strife, bitterness, wrath, anger, and clamor, and evil-speaking, lying, false accusing, persecuting the innocent; and live in drunkenness, bloody-mindedness, hateful, and hating one another, desperate, rash, hasty, perverse, unruly, disorderly, rebellious, unmerciful, cruel, hard-hearted, fierce, despisers, wrathful, obstinate, willful, stubborn, full of threats, revilings, haters of God; you love not enemies, you honor not all men, you do not love the brotherhood, you do not depart from sin and evil, you do not fear God, nor is it like that you should honor the king.

Therefore, all people from the highest to the lowest, it is a warning to you all to depart from sin, and all manner of evil whatsoever. You that live in adultery, idolatry, fornication, uncleanness, whoredom, cursing, swearing, drunkenness, lying, sporting, and pleasures, and in cozening, cheating, deceiving, and defrauding one another, dissembling, reproaching, persecuting one another with tongue or hand, backbiting, or slandering, or false accusing one another, rage, malice and hatred, and envying one another; fear God, and depart from those things, and come into the chasteness, and temperance, and sobriety, and truth, and righteousness, and meekness, and lowliness of mind, out of the high-mindedness, and to love the brotherhood, honor all men, fear God, and then you honor the king.

For as you live in sin and evil, you honor not all men, you love not the brotherhood, you fear not God, you honor not the king; that is from the Lord God to you all, you dishonor men, you dishonor the brotherhood, you dishonor the king, you cast the fear of God behind your backs, and his law also, who live in sin and evil, though you talk ever so much of your honoring the king, we will never believe you, that you honor him while you live in sin, for that is it which is to his dishonor and reproach, and will bring the judgments of God upon the land.

So while you live in the wrath, and revenge, swearing, cursing, blaspheming the name of God, and taking it in vain, and professing him in words, but in works denying of him, living in lying, deceit, and hypocrisy, and dissimulation, in unrighteousness, injustice, and iniquity, in pride, idleness, and fullness of bread, in drunkenness, riotousness, and vain pleasures and delights, (which causes you to forget God), and all manner of cozening, cheating, deceit, defrauding, puffed up, heady, high-minded, ambitious, and overreaching one another in bargaining, to overthrow one another to make yourselves rich; we do say, and know, and believe that you neither fear God, nor honor the king, nor love enemies, nor love the brotherhood, nor honor all men, who are so wicked, and malicious and envious against men.

All that are persecutors about religion, church-worship, ordinances, ministers' maintenance, and their traditions, which the apostles never did so in the primitive times, neither did Christ ever give any such command; these fear not God that do so, neither do they honor all men that do persecute them about religion and worship, and cast them into prison, nor love enemies, nor love brotherhood, nor fear God, nor Christ, nor honor the king; for Christ gave them no such command, as you may read in the scriptures of truth.

Fifthly, and all those who persecute people about observing of days, and not observing of days, meats, and drinks, which stand in the will-worship, as the apostle speaks of, 2 Col, who neglect the body.
