TO ALL BISHOPS, PREACHERS, TEACHERS, AND PRIESTS IN CHRISTENDOM - Continued-

And in Luke 5, ‘Jesus entered into the ship which was Simon Peter's and bid him thrust off a little from the land, and Jesus sat down, and taught the people out of the ship; and when he had done speaking, he said unto Simon, launch out into the deep, and let down your nets to make a draught, and they inclosed a multitude of fish, though before they had been toiling all night, and catched nothing; so that their net was ready to break, and they beckoned to their partners which were in the ship, that they should come to help them, who came, and they filled both their ships, so that they began to sink. Now when Simon Peter saw it, he fell down at Jesus' feet, saying, Lord go from me, I am a sinful man, for he was utterly astonished, and all that were with him, for the draught of fish which they had taken, and so was also James and John, the sons of Zebedee, which were partners with Simon, then Jesus said unto Simon, fear not, from henceforth you shall catch men, and when they brought the fishes to land, they forsook all and followed him,’ namely, Jesus. Here you may see what manner of men were Christ's disciples, fishermen;. ‘and Christ preached in Peter's ship, and Peter the fisherman did catch many men according to Christ's words, as when he converted three thousand at one sermon, as in Acts 2:41.

And the magistrates perceived that Peter and John were unlearned men, and could not read letters, but they took knowledge they had been with Jesus, who is the true teacher, as in Acts 4:13. If they had been in your days, would not you have mocked at them, as the letter [scripture] professing [believing] Jews did? Do not you do the same to those speaking by his Spirit now? ‘Peter and the other disciples did abide with Christ: and after Jesus was risen, there were together Simon Peter, and Thomas called Didimus, and Nathaniel of Cana, in Galilee, and the sons of Zebedee, and two other of his disciples, and Simon Peter said to them, I go a fishing, and they said unto him, we go also with you; and they went straightway and entered into a ship, and that night caught they nothing, but when the morning was come, Jesus stood on the shore, and the disciples knew not that it was Jesus, and Jesus said unto them, sirs, have you any meat, and they answered him no; then he said unto them, cast out the net on the right side of the ship, and you shall find; so they cast out, and they were not able to draw it for the multitude of fish; therefore, said the disciple whom Jesus loved, it is the Lord; and when Simon Peter heard that it was Jesus, he girt his fisher's coat unto him; for he was naked, and cast himself into the sea, but the other disciples came in the ship, for they were not far from land, but about two hundred cubits, and they drew the net with fishes to land.’

Now, you may see here the apostles what plain men they were, and Peter was in his fisher's coat after Christ was risen, who had been a preacher of Christ a great while from his conversion, for several years, until Christ was crucified, who saw Christ's miracle before he was converted, and now again after he was risen, as in John 21. And so Peter and John, and the other disciples, had caught nothing; neither when they who had been toiling all night before their conversion, nor after Christ had risen; but at Christ's words they caught many fish, even to their astonishment; and that might let them see the truth of Christ's words, that a sparrow should not fall to the ground without the will of the Father, so a fish could not be caught in the net without the will of Christ, by whom all things were made.

But here you may see that Peter and John, and the sons of Zebedee, they were fishermen before they were converted, and after Christ was risen, and were partners, and went a fishing together, as in Luke 5 and John 21. Today, would not fisherman turned-preachers and  disciples be detestable to you ministers, as they were to the high priests, and Pharisees, and council, and the rulers who cast them in prison, and commanded them to speak no more in the name of Jesus, as you may see in Acts 4 and 5 and consulted to slay them, but that Gamaliel moderated the priests and council; and so when they had scourged the apostles they let them go. So here was the fulfilling of Christ's words, who told them before, that for his namesake they should be had before the rulers, and scourged. And now consider, you that are called ministers and priests, and others, are you found in the spirit of the apostles, or in the spirit of those that scourged? Is there not lacking a Gamaliel among you to moderate you? Did not the Puritan Congretationalist priests and rulers in New-England lack a Gamaliel among them, to moderate them, when they murdered four Quakers in Boston; and have not the papists and protestants lacked a Gamaliel among them, who have caused so many to suffer since the apostles' days, and to say unto you, ‘if it be of God it shall stand, let these men alone, for fear that you be found fighters against God?’

Again, consider you priests and others, and measure yourselves, who say the scripture is your rule, whether you are in the priests' spirit, and Saul's unconverted spirit, with his packet of letters, and from that spirit gave forth such commissions to your souls, to haul and bind the true christians, and bring them to prison; are not such persecutors of Christ's seed, Acts 9: ‘Why are you persecuting me?’ and after Saul was converted and became a Paul, did not the unconverted priests and Jews seek to destroy him, and to stone him, as may be seen in Acts 14 though they were believers in the letter [scriptures] of the old testament, just as you are of the letter [scriptures] of the new testament.

And Paul said, how the chief priests and elders could bear him witness how he persecuted the churches, Acts 22 and Acts 23, it may be seen how the high priests and others were vehement in accusing of him, and in Acts 23, the high priests and chief of the Jews appeared against Paul; and above forty men had bound themselves with an oath, they would neither eat nor drink until they had destroyed him, and this they told to the chief priests and the elders, and said unto them, ‘now therefore, you (namely, the chief priests and elders, and the council), signify to the chief captain, that he bring him forth unto you to-morrow, as though you would know something more perfectly of Paul.’ So see the design of the Jews and chief priests here to kill Paul, which God prevented; for he had been in profession with them while he was a persecutor; but now he was a professor of Christ. See what a madness was in them against Paul, just as there was madness in Paul against others before his conversion. Now see if you are not in the same spirit and way of madness, being professors of Christ but without possession of his spirit. Are you not like Saul and the chief priests, and not like converted Paul; which of these ways are you in? Does not your fruits speak of your position? Measure yourselves, and try by the scripture, which you say is your rule, which spirit you are guided by.

And in 1 Cor 9. ‘Who ever goes to war at his own expense? Who plants a vineyard and does not eat of its fruit? Or who tends a flock and does not drink of the milk of the flock?’ And ‘You shall not muzzle an ox while it treads out the grain..’ he who plows should plow in hope, and he who threshes in hope should be partaker of his hope. If we have sown spiritual things for you, is it a great thing if we reap your material things? If others are partakers of this right over you, are we not even more? Nevertheless, said the apostle, we have not used this right, but endure all things lest we hinder the gospel of Christ’

Now these are the scriptures you priests quote to justify your salaries; but come, measure yourselves; how can you sow spiritual things, as the apostles did, when you admit you do not have the same spirit and power the apostles had; and without true sowing, how can you expect reap their carnal things in payment? For the apostle had the spiritual flail to thresh God's wheat out of the chaff, that is: the corruptions, the body of death, and the body of sin, which you plead for term of life; and the apostles had the spirit to war the spiritual war, but since you do not have the same spirit they had, you cannot war the spiritual war. The apostles  had the spirit of God to plant God's vineyard, and you that don’t have the same spirit that they had cannot plant such vineyards, and therefore cannot eat the spiritual fruit. The apostles with the Holy Ghost, which made them all ministers and overseers, and with the Holy Spirit’s supplied words, fed the flock, so they could eat of the milk. The apostles treaded the corn out of the chaff, saying to some that they were elected before the world began; but you who deny having the same spirit the apostles had, you cannot tread out the corn, and eat of the milk of that flock, which the Holy Ghost oversees. The apostles had the heavenly plough to turn up the fallow ground, that did not bear fruit unto God; but though the apostles had this heavenly plough with which to plough, and the heavenly flail with which to thresh out the wheat, yet he told the church at Corinth, he had not used his right among them, though he had been there among them a year and six months.

And now you that say you have not the same power and Holy Ghost the apostles had; look what carnal ploughs, flails, and vineyards and flocks you have; and do not you plough large furrows upon the righteous' backs, and your own ground lies fallow, full of thistles and briars, and your vineyards waste, and you run to other vineyards, and have no fruit in your vineyards, nor milk in your flocks, but what you seize by laws; and must not the mouth of the ox be muzzled that does not tread out the corn? But the apostle, would not use his right, (who did tread out the corn), among them he had begotten and converted. But you will claim a right among them, despite the fact that you have neither begotten, nor converted; instead you them cast into prison, if they will not give you maintenance; so, where did the apostles ever do such a thing? Measure yourselves, and your conformance to the scriptures, which you say is your rule?

And further the apostle said, ‘those who minister the holy things eat of the things of the temple, and those who serve at the altar partake of the offerings of the altar? Even so the Lord has commanded that those who preach the gospel should live from the gospel.


But I have used none of these things, nor have I written these things that it should be done so to me; for it would be better for me to die than that anyone should make my boasting void. For if I preach the gospel, I have nothing to boast of, for necessity is laid upon me; yes, woe is me if I do not preach the gospel! For if I do this willingly, I have a reward; but if against my will, I have been entrusted with a stewardship. What is my reward then? That when I preach the gospel, I may present the gospel of Christ without charge, that I may not abuse my authority in the gospel.’[Unlike so many, we do not peddle the word of God for profit.] And now consider you priests in Christendom, and measure yourselves here with the apostle. Have you nothing for preaching the gospel, and yet woe unto you, if you do not preach the gospel? And have you kept the gospel free without charge? Is this your reward? Have not you abused the authority of the gospel? Let your fruits speak, who say the scripture is your rule for your life and doctrine, therefore try your life and doctrine by it, and see if your gospel is free without charge, as Paul's was; and if it had been so, then you had not spoiled people's goods, and cast them into prison unto death. Do not your deeds testify otherwise?

And the apostle said, ‘Though I am free from all men, yet have I made myself servant unto all men, that I might win the more.’ See, he did not make himself a master, but a servant; try yourselves by this, and see whether this scripture is your rule or not, and whether you are not judged by it, and Christ, and the apostles?

And in 2 Cor 9, which is the scripture you priests use to bring to prove your maintenance by, where the apostle speaks concerning ministering to saints [who were the poor saints in Jerusalem,* not the apostles, pastors, and evangelists] and where he thanks God for his unspeakable gift, and this is nothing to serve for you to prove your forced maintenance by, and to force a maintenance from people by; for the apostle says, ‘As every man is persuaded in his heart, so let him give, not grudgingly, but of a cheerful heart, for God loveth a cheerful giver; [He has dispersed abroad, he has given to the poor; his righteousness endures forever]’ so here the church was left to their freedom, [even to give to the poor]; but you who are not poor, dare apply this scripture to yourselves], neither trust God nor the church for your bellies; but try yourselves and your practice by this scripture.

*The tithes of the Jews were provide for the poor, the widows, and the orphans. When the poor joined the early Christian church, they were immediately cut off from any assistance by the Jewish priests. The poor were many in the early Church; that is why Paul is on record twice as asking for a collection to be taken for the poor saints in Jerusalem. But this money was not for himself, nor for the preachers.

And again, in Phil 4, you bring this where the apostle said, ‘When I was in Thessalonica, you sent once and again for my necessity, not that I desired a gift, but I desired the fruit, which may farther abound to your account; now I have received all, and have plenty; I was even filled, after that I had received of Epaphroditus that which came from you, an odour that smelleth sweet, a sacrifice acceptable and pleasant to God, and my God shall fulfil all your necessities through his riches and glory in Jesus Christ.

Now measure yourselves by this scripture, you preachers, ministers, and priests of Christendom; see if you are equal with the apostle, who did not ask for a gift.* Your solicitations, even forcing maintenance, are nothing close to the apostle, who only desired the fruits of his vineyard which he had planted [as in love, peace, joy], which was as a sweet smelling sacrifice to God. For Paul had learned to be full and to be hungry, and to be abased, and to abound, and also to lack; he had learned this lesson of Christ, which you have not yet learned at your schools, and you are never likely to learn it from Christ, particularly when you say there is no hearing of his voice these days.

 *A salary is not a gift. A collection is not a gift. Tithes are not a gift. To ask, request, or require destroys the concept of a gift. A gift must be totally unsolicited, to be a real gift.

And in 1 Thes  4:11, the apostle writes, and said, ‘aspire to lead a quiet life, to mind your own business, and to work with your own hands, as we commanded you, that you may walk properly toward those who are outside, and that you may lack nothing.’ And in 2 Thes 3. ‘And we have confidence in the Lord concerning you, both that you do and will do the things we command you,.. For you yourselves know how you ought to follow us, for we were not disorderly among you; nor did we eat anyone’s bread free of charge, but worked with labor and toil night and day, that we might not be a burden to any of you, not because we do not have authority, but to make ourselves an example of how you should follow us. For even when we were with you, we commanded you this: If anyone will not work, neither shall he eat. For we hear that there are some who walk among you in a disorderly manner, not working at all, but are busybodies. Now those who are such we command [mark!] and exhort through our Lord Jesus Christ that they work in quietness and eat their own bread.’

Come, all you priests and teachers in Christendom, measure yourselves here, and try yourselves whether you observe the apostles' command here, and have him for your example, and whether you are the successors of him in his example.The apostle chose to work day and night, rather than he would be a burden to the church; but are not you a burden, insisting on salaries, even imprisoning and spoiling people's goods that you do not preach to? How dare you say the scripture is your rule, and that you are successors to the apostles, when your lives and practice speak quite contrary? You are to study to be quiet, and with quietness to eat your own bread, and not another man's, (and especially those who you do no work for; for you would think it an unreasonable thing for a tradesman to come unto you for money for his ware, from whom you had no work, and force you to give him money for it, or to throw you into prison? Then is it not as strange for you to ask people for money, that have had none of your ware, and if they will not give it you throw them into prison?) Is this your studying to be quiet, and laboring with your own hands, and eating your own bread with quietness? I think not; and does not the apostle say, ‘if anyone does not obey our word in this epistle, note that person and do not keep company with him, that he may be ashamed.’ mark this all you that disobey Christ's and the apostle's command also.

In 1 Tim 3:1. This is a true saying, ‘if a man desire the office of a bishop, he desires a good work:’ so now you bishops and elders, who say the scripture is your rule, come and be tried by this scripture, you that desire this office, this good work.

First. ‘A bishop must be unreprovable and blameless; are you so?

Secondly. ‘He must be the husband of one wife, watching, sober, modest, given to hospitality, apt to teach:’ now mark, are you found in these practises? let your fruits and the consciences of people speak.

Thirdly. ‘Not given to wine, no striker, [mark, no striker nor persecutor,] not given to filthy lucre, but gentle, no flatterers, not covetous;’ so consider, you bishops in Christendom; are you not strikers, not given to wine or filthy lucre [money]? Are you gentle and not covetous, nor fighters? Let the country and your consciences, and your fruits speak, and God and the scripture judge.

Further, a bishop must be one that can rule his own house honestly, having children under obedience with all honesty; for if he cannot rule his own house, how shall he take care for the church of God. He must not be a young in the Lord, for fear that he becomes puffed up in pride; he must also be well reported of them which are without, for fear that he fall into rebuke and snare of the devil.

And now come try yourselves, you bishops, by these scriptures; do you rule your own house well? Are your children under obedience with all honesty? Look into your families; are you not fighters or strikers? Are you not covetous, and given to filthy lucre? If you cannot rule your own house well, how can you rule the church of God? Let the prisons testifiy if you priests and ministers are not covetous; and are you and they of a good report without? How can that be, when you take people's goods from them, and cast them into prison, that do not hear you? Does not this bring a bad report upon you?

And does it not show that you are puffed up, and that you are fallen under rebuke, and the snare of the devil; let your consciences, and your fruits, and the prisons, and them that are without, and the scripture speak, which you call your rule to be tried by, and God and Christ judge.

Likewise, deacons must be grave and honest, come, let the deacons be tried by scripture also, not double-tongues, (they must not be double-tongued, take notice) not given to wine nor filthy lucre, (mark the deacons), having the mystery of faith in a pure conscience; likewise their wives must be honest, not evil-speakers, but sober and faithful in all things; come, try your wives here, you bishops and deacons. And likewise, you deacons, you are to be the husband of one wife, as the bishops must, to be the husband of one wife, and such as can rule their children well, and their own household; so mark, and look into your households, and among your children, and see how they are ruled. But you bishops, whether papists or others, who not only tie yourselves from marriage, but also forbid it to priests and bishops, how do you succeed the apostles or those primitive bishops therein? do not you do worse? Is not that contrary to the apostle's doctrine, who calls it the doctrine of devils to forbid marriage, 1 Tim 4:1-3, and does not the apostle tell you, you must hold the mystery of faith in a pure conscience; and is it not your principle, that people must carry about them the body of death and sin to the grave? Then you cannot hold the mystery of faith in a pure conscience, which is the gift of God, that purifies the heart, in pleading for that which does defile it, term of life.

And the apostle said, ‘let them first be proved, (namely, bishops and deacons), then let them minister, if they be found blameless.’

Now you bishops and deacons, are you blameless from filthy lucre, and from covetousness, and no fighters or persecutors? Are you blameless concerning your children, wives, and families? Are you blameless to them that are without? Are you blameless concerning your tongues? Are you blameless in being able to teach, and in watching, and sobriety, and hospitality, and blameless of evil speaking, and not given to much wine, and unreprovable in these things? For the apostle said, first you must be proved concerning these things, and then to be approved if you are found blameless of these things, else by the apostle's doctrine you are not to minister, and you that are found in these things, cannot be the successors to the apostles.

So let your houses, your families, your lives, your fruits, and the jails, and the scriptures speak, and the people that are without, and God and Christ judge, whether you are clear from these things which the apostle reproves.

And the apostle who desired others to follow him, as he followed Christ, and mark them for their examples, who coveted no man's silver, gold, or apparel, and eat no man's bread for nothing; but can you priests say so, that have taken goods from people, and cast them in prison, and have done no work for them, and they had nothing from you; is not this eating other's bread for nothing? But the apostle tells Timothy be an example unto them that believed, (that is, to the church), he was to be an example in word and in conduct, and an example in love, and an example in spirit, and an example in pureness, for in so doing he both saved himself and them that heard him. 1 Tim 4:12. So try yourselves here by the apostle's command, and see what example you give.

And in 1 Tim 6:5 the apostle said to Timothy of such who suppose that godliness is a means of gain, ‘from such withdraw yourself;’ What do you think of this you preachers? If you had no financial gain, would you preach to anyone? Then do you not use your godliness to obtain financial gain. From such persons the man of God must be separated; and is not this to measure religion by riches? Try yourselves by this scripture.

But the apostle said, ‘godliness is great gain,’ if a man be content with what he has, for nothing we brought into the world, and it is certain we shall carry nothing out, and therefore, having food and raiment, let us therewith be content; for those who will be rich in this world fall into temptation and snares, and many foolish and noisome lusts, which drown men in perdition and destruction.

And now measure yourselves you bishops and priests, and professors, and see if you be not fallen into these temptations and snares, and many noisome lusts, and drowned in perdition and destruction, who have disobeyed the command of Christ, which is, ‘freely you have received, freely give;’ so there is not to be a looking after gain then, which the man of God is to separate himself from them that do: ‘for the desire of money is the root of all evil, which while some lusted after, they erred from the faith, and pierced themselves through with many sorrows; but you man of God flee these things, and follow after righteousness, faith, love, patience, and meekness.’

Now here you may see what the man of God must flee from, and what he must follow after; but come measure yourselves you that teach for money, and are lovers of money, and others, do you not possess the root of all evil, lusting after money in your hearts. Are not you pierced through with many sorrows? Hav not you erred from the faith that gives victory? Do you not even say, victory from the body of sin and imperfection on this side the grave is impossible? Have you not erred from the command of Christ, which said, ‘freely you have received, (without charge) freely give, (without pay)?’ Come try yourselves by the scripture, which you say is the rule, whether your actions are not found disobedient to Christ's command, and the apostle's doctrine and charge to Timothy, the man of God.

And in 2 Tim 3, the apostle tells Timothy, that in the last times perilous days should come, that men should be lovers of their own selves, covetous, proud, boasters, cursed speakers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, without natural affection, truce-breakers, false accusers, intemperate, fierce despisers of them which are good, [mark, fierce despiser sof them which are good,] traitors, heady, high minded, lovers of pleasures more than lovers of God, [mark,] having a form of godliness, but have denied the power thereof, therefore, from such turn away, [mark,] for of this sort are they which creep into houses, leading silly women captive, laden with sins, and led away with divers lusts, ever learning, but never able to come to the knowledge of the truth; and as Jannes and Jambres withstood Moses, so do these resist the truth, men of corrupt minds, reprobate concerning the faith; but they shall proceed no further, for their madness shall be manifest to all, as their's was.

Now come you priests and professors, and all others called christians, measure yourselves and try yourselves by these scriptures; are not you lovers of yourselves, and covetous, and proud boasters. Do you not curse and disobey parents? Are not you unthankful and unholy, and do not you plead for unholiness as long as you live, and say there is no overcoming the body of sin here? Are you not covetousness of money, even seizing people's goods for tithes? Are not you to be turned away from, according to the apostle's doctrine? Have not you been false accusers, and intemperate, and fierce despisers of those who are good? Are not without natural affection, when you imprison so many for your bellies, and make so many fatherless and widows, such as you did no work for? Is not this without natural affections? Do you not put on a  show of godliness, professing Christ's, the prophets', and apostles' words? Do not you deny being in the  power and spirit of the Holy Ghost that the prophets and apostles were in? Do not you keep people always learning, and leading them, so that they may be always paying you, yet they are never able to come to the knowledge of the truth? Is not this a sad learning, and a sad leading? Are not you and your people laden with sins and many lusts? Do you not tell the people that both you and they must carry the body of sin, death, and imperfections to the grave? Are you and your people not burdened with your body of sin? Is not this a heavy load to carry their entire life to the grave, and there to be buried altogether with it, where there is no repentance? Do not you keep your people always learning, always under your teaching, that they may be always paying, and resist the truth, like Jannes and Jambres, who resisted Moses? Are you not reprobates concerning the faith that should give victory over the load of sin? Is not your madness seen here, as Jannes' and Jambres' was? Is not the apostle's doctrine to be obeyed now, as then, who said ‘turn away from all such,’ though you may have a form of godliness, yet deny the power thereof, as they did in the apostle's time. For has not the highmindedness appeared, and your loving pleasures been manifest to those who are in the power of God, and turn away from you? Try yourselves by the scripture, and let God and Christ judge.

And the apostle said, 2 Tim 4 he instructs Timothy to preach the word ‘in season, and out of season.’ Come, professors and priests, measure yourselves by this; in season, may be you will say, is when the sun shines, and out of season, is when there is a storm; but there is more need to look after the sheep when the storm is, than when the sun shines; for when there is a storm, is there then not most need? So try your practice by the scripture. Demas forsook Paul to embrace the world; and have not many done like Demas herein? Let that of God in your conscience speak, and God and Christ judge.

And the apostle tells Timothy ‘flee from all youthful lusts, and follow after righteousness, faith, love, peace with all them that call on the name of the Lord with a pure heart.’ Come, try yourselves by this scripture, and if you were found according to Paul's exhortation, it would be well; but do you minister to such people as call upon the Lord with a pure heart? Do you believe there are any such people now, who call upon the Lord with a pure heart? Is this not contrary to your principle and belief, who say people must carry a body of sin and death to the grave. We never read in the scriptures that ever Christ or his apostles preached up such a doctrine, that people must carry a body of sin and death, and imperfection to the grave; instead they preached up holiness, pureness and perfection; and told them to be perfect, and to follow the apostles, and to walk as Christ walked.

And Paul writes to Titus of the qualifications of bishops and elders, how that ‘they must be unreprovable, the husband of one wife, having faithful children, who not accused of dissipation; [mark,] neither insubbordinate; for a bishop must be unblamebable as God's steward, not forwardnot self-willed, not quick-tempered, not given to wine, not violent, not greedy for money [filthy lucre];’ measure yourselves with your rule, the scripture, and see whether you are not given to filthy lucre; ‘for,’ said the apostle, ‘there are many disobedient and idle [vain] talkers, chiefly of the circumcision.’ Now this circumcision was a command of God; this outward circumcision they cry up, and leave the circumcision of the heart; but the apostle said, ‘whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre's sake; one of themselves, even one of their own prophets said, the Cretians are always liars, evil beasts, slow-bellies; this witness is true, therefore reprove them sharply, that they may be found in the faith.’

So you see here, what and who must be reproved, and how these money motivated prophets can speak one against another, and call them liars, evil beasts, and slow-bellies; and is it not the practice of such now, that are found in these steps, and such now as teach for filthy lucre, as can cry up outward commands and the letter, and make a mock of the spirit's moving and leadings. So we see these could cry up circumcision, an outward command, for filthy lucre's sake, as many do now talk of the letter [the Bible] , but cannot endure to talk of the light, and Christ being manifested within, and will not come to the light for fear that it should reprove them; but it is their condemnation. For they profess they know God, but by works thay deny him, and are abominable, and to every good work reprobate, see Tit 1 and examine yourselves by it. And Paul tells him, ‘in all things showing yourself to be a pattern of good works; in doctrine showing integrity, reverence, incorruptibility.’ So now, do you prove yours, and see whether you are found such examples in such works and uncorrupt doctrine; for as James said in Chapter. 2 ‘as the body without the spirit is dead, even so faith without works is dead,’ Now this faith purifies the heart and gives victory, and without it people cannot please God; and this faith works by love.

So you that deny overcoming sin and evil, and the body of sin and death on this side the grave, have not this true faith which gives victory, and in 1 Pet 5 ‘he writes to the elders who was also an elder, that they should feed the flock of God, not by constraint, but willingly; not for filthy lucre, but of a ready mind, not as though you were lords over God's heritage, but that you be examples to the flock.’ Now mark, and come and try yourselves, you priests and teachers of Christendom, and elders; do not you feed your flocks for filthy lucre's sake? Are there any among you that don’t act as lords and masters over God's heritage; yes, and have the title father, teacher, master, though it is contrary to Christ's command, as in Mat 23:6. Those who are in the Holy Ghost, are in the succession to the church-fellowship that was in the primitive times, and are in the succession of praying in the Holy Ghost, and comfort of it, and the succeeders of feeding the flock, and the overseeing it in the Holy Ghost that the apostles were in. Those who are the spiritual successors of the apostles, are in the Holy Ghost that leads them into all truth, as it led the apostles into all truth.

And in Ezek 22:25-31, to the end, ‘there is a conspiracy of the prophets in the midst thereof, like a roaring lion ravening the prey, they have devoured souls, they have taken the riches and precious things, they have made many widows in the midst thereof, (namely, Jerusalem), her priests have broken my law, and have defiled my holy things, they have put no difference between the holy and the profane, the clean and the unclean,'.. and I am profaned among them, her prophets have daubed with untempered mortar, seeing vanity and divining lies, saying, thus said the Lord, when the Lord has not spoken; the people of the land have been violently oppressed by spoiling and robbing, and they vexed the poor and the needy; yes, they vexed the poor against his right; I sought for a man among them that should make up the hedge, and stand in the gap before me for the land, that I should not destroy it, but I found none.'

Now consider this, you priests, prophets, and professors in Christendom, try yourselves, and see if you are not found in this spirit as these priests and prophets were in under the law? Have you not broken the gospel and the command of Christ, as they broke the law? Have you not made many widows and fatherless by casting in prison for your tithes? Have not you said, thus said the Lord, and the Lord never spoke to you? Have not you been like ravening lions for your prey? have not you vexed the poor and needy, and stranger by oppressing and spoiling? and is not this contrary to the gospel and Christ's command, who said, ‘freely you have received (with charge), freely give (without pay).’ So measure yourselves and your practice by this scripture.

And Christ said John 10, ‘an hireling will flee because he is an hireling, and cares not for the sheep.’ And has not this been fulfilled among the hirelings in this our age? and Christ said, ‘the thief comes not, but to steal, to kill, and to destroy, but I am come that you may have life, and have it more abundantly, and all that come before me are thieves and robbers, but the sheep did not hear them, [mark,] did not hear them, and I am the door of the sheep, if by me any man enter in, he shall be saved, and go in and out and find pasture.’ Therefore, come now and try yourselves by Christ's own words, who is the good shepherd, and has laid down his life for his sheep; and therefore what do you say about all them that have killed and destroyed about religion since the apostles' days, and say they have not heard the voice of Christ? Do not these people steal their words from their neighbors, and run, and God never sent them? Are Christ's sheep supposed to listen to thieves and robbers? Does not Christ enlightens every man that comes into the world? Is Christ not the door, and must not everyone that enters into his pasture, enter in by the light, or else how can they see the door without the light, which comes from him who is the life? For it is said, ‘the light which shines in the heart, gives the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Christ Jesus,' who is the door.’ So they cannot enter into Christ the door, except by the light that gives the knowledge. So try yourselves, all of you that say Christ has not enlightened every man that comes into the world with a heavenly and divine light; how can you possilby see to enter the door without this light? See if by your denial of this light whether or not you are keeping people yet in darkness, among the hirelings, and thieves, and robbers, and from the good shepherd that lays down his life for his sheep, and are climbing up some other way? But Christ's sheep hear his voice and follow him, who is the same today as yesterday, and so forever, though you say there is no hearing his voice now today.

And the apostle said, ‘let no man judge you in meats, or drinks, or in respect of a holy day, or new-moons, or Sabbath-days, which are but a shadow of things to come, but the body is of Christ.’ Now mark, for people to contend about such things, is to contend about shadows of things to come, and that which the kingdom of God stands not in, but the body is of Christ. So Christ and his body is to be minded, who is the substance of such things, that were figures, types or shadows of him; and he is to be minded, who is the head, and the substance, and life of all.

And further the apostle said, ‘let no man beguile you of your reward in a voluntary humility and worshipping of angels, intruding into those things which he hath not seen, vainly puffed up by his fleshly mind, and not holding the head,’ ...

Now consider this, all of you who say that there is no hearing of God's or Christ's voice now days, and say that you don't the same power and spirit the apostles had.

Therefore, come try yourselves by the scripture, which you say is your rule, are not you those who bear rule by your position with your pretended humbleness of mind, advancing yourselves into Christ, the prophets' and apostles' words, which you never saw in that spirit that gave them forth, and rashly puffed up in your fleshly minds. Now consider whether people must acknowledge the authority of those like the apostle cried against in his day, now in our days; I say, should those who hold Christ Jesus the head, to be their teacher, and their prophet, and their shepherd, whether they must support such as were cried against, yes, or no? The apostle further said, ‘if you are dead with Christ from the ordinances of the world, why are you subject to the ordinances of the world as though you are still living in the world: touch not, taste not, handle not, which all perish with the using, after the commandments and doctrines of men, which things indeed have a show of wisdom in a voluntary religion, &c. [mark,] a voluntary religion, then it is not the religion that is pure, which James speaks of.

Therefore, should we not in our age follow the apostles as the saints did in their age, and not to touch, taste, nor handle the doctrines of men, or commandments of men, which will perish with the using, though they may have a show of wisdom in a voluntary religion. Those who are not dead with Christ may follow such traditions and ordinances of men, and touch, taste and handle that which perishes with the using; but those who are dead and risen with Christ, cannot follow such things, as in Col 2. So try yourselves by this scripture whether you follow the apostles' doctrine, or such as he forbad.

And in 1 Tim 4, the apostle said, ‘the spirit speaks expressly that some shall depart from the faith in the latter times, and shall give heed to the spirit of error, and doctrines of devils, which speak lies in hypocrisy, having their consciences seared as with an hot iron, forbidding to marry, and commanding to abstain from meats, which God has created to be received with thanksgiving of them that believe and know the truth; for every creature of God is good, and ought not to be refused, if it be received with thanksgiving, for it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer.’

Now come and try yourselves, all of you that forbid meats [meatless Friday's] and marriages [priests cannot marry], are not you those who the apostle prophesied of, and the spirit spoke of, that have departed from the true faith that the apostles were in and are in the spirit of error and doctrine of devils, which speak lies in hypocrisy, having your consciences burned with an hot iron. Are you not out of the belief and the truth that the apostles were in, and so see not the end of God's creating of his creatures, to be received with thanksgiving, who said, every creature of God is good, and nothing ought to be refused, if it be received with thanksgiving, for it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer; so not sanctified to you that forbid them, neither do you know the end of God's creating them. ‘Do not be deceived, God is not mocked; for whatever a man sows, that he will also reap. For he who sows to his flesh will of the flesh reap corruption, but he who sows to the Spirit will of the Spirit reap everlasting life. Gal 6:7-8.

Now come try yourselves all you teachers in Christendom, and look into your fields or vineyards, and see what you have there sown, and see what a great crop of corruptions and all manner of evils you may reap out of them; for you saying you have not the same spirit, and plough, and flail, and faith that the apostles had, then you cannot sow unto the spirit, by which you might reap life eternal; and though you may have all the scriptures from Genesis to the end of the Revelation, if you have not the same spirit as they had that gave them forth, you cannot sow to the spirit, neither are you successors of the prophets and apostles, except you are in the spirit that gave forth the scriptures. And the apostle said ‘let every man prove his own work, and then shall he have rejoicing in himself only, and not in another.’ So now try yourselves you priests and teachers, and prove your own work, and see what joy you have in it; to rejoice in another man's work, the prophets' or apostles', [taking joy by reading the scriptures about their joy] is not to rejoice in your own work, the work that God has wrought in you; and if you come to this, you must come to the light (as Christ said), to see whether your works are wrought in God, that you may have joy in him, and in yourselves in his spirit. Again the apostle said, ‘Let him who is taught the word share in all good things with him who teaches.’ Gal 6:6.

Site Editor's Comment: To be taught in the word, is not to be taught what the scriptures say, or someone's opinion of their meaning; but to be taught as the Spirit of God gives appropriate words to the speaker, tailored to the ears and hearts of his listeners. To be taught in the word, is to be taught by someone who, under control of the Holy Spirit, speaks the Word of God, as heard from God, as commanded by God to them to be spoken, at that specific time, to those specific listeners; as is in 1 Cor 2:13, 'this is what we speak, not in words taught us by human wisdom, but in words taught by the Spirit, expressing spiritual truths in spiritual words. We would naturally want to share everything we had with someone who was truly teaching us words he was given by God to speak to us.

Now is not this the scripture which you bring for your maintenance? but what is this to prove your forced maintenance by, and to cast people in prison, if they will not give you tithes, or midsummer-dues, Easter reckonings, and the like; this was spoken to them that were taught in the word, not to them whom they did not teach, and to force from them: but you use to say, why do not they come, and hear? they may come if they will; did the apostles force a maintenance from any that did not hear them, and say, they might come if they would, and we have a law to compel it from you; if so, where is it written in the scripture, which you say is the rule? but in this your practice, have not you thrown away Christ's law and command, who tells freely to give, as they had received. But the apostle said, ‘he that is taught in the word,’ he does not say, he that is taught in the letter; John said, ‘in the beginning was the word, and in him was life, and this life was the light of men, and this is the true light that lights every man that comes into the world:’ and you that deny this light, do not you deny the teaching of the word, and call the light natural? and then in your account is not the word natural? therefore, how can you have the good things communicated to you, which do not communicate the good and everlasting things to people; and you see that the apostle would not receive of them that he did teach the good word to, but labored with his hands, that he might keep the gospel without charge, and show an example to all that succeeded him, neither would he use his power; but have you not gone beyond this power, and abused it; try yourselves. 1 Cor 9, Gal 6. And in Luke 8. ‘and Joanna wife of Herod's steward, and Susannah, with many others, which ministered unto Christ of their substance.’ Now this scripture, which you also quote to justify your gain of filthy lucre, proves nothing for your solicited maintenance, for what they did, they did it freely. Christ never made a law to force people, or ever passed the collection bag around, or ever asked anyone, to give him and his disciples maintenance; if you say he did, show where it is written. Again, Habakkuk said in Hab 2:9, ‘Woe to him that coveteth an evil covetousness to his house.’ And therefore, take heed of this woe coming upon you, all you that covet an evil covetousness to your house, that you may set your nests on high, who have coveted and taken other people's goods that you have done no work for, be sure it will, except you repent. And in Amos 6, is it not said, ‘woe to them that chant, or sing to the sound of the viol, and invent to themselves instruments of music like David, that drink wine in bowls, and anoint themselves with the chief ointments, but they are not grieved for the affliction of Joseph.’ Now consider this you priests and professors, and others, and try your practice by this scripture, and whether you are not found in these practises, and putting far away the evil day, and approach the seat of iniquity? Are not you them that stretch yourselves upon your beds? Therefore see what judgments the Lord threatened should come upon such; and do you think the like judgments will not come upon you, that are found in their practises.

Therefore, do not profess the scriptures to be your rule, except you walk in the spirit, which was both leader and rule, to the holy men of God that gave it forth, and according to their example that gave it forth in life and practice; for if you do not, you are judged and reproved by the holy scripture, and the spirit of God and Christ, by which they were given forth.

George Fox

Worcester Prison, the 11 th month, 1674
